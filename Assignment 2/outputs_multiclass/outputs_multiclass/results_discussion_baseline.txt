
RESULTS — BASELINE CNN

We trained a baseline convolutional neural network for multiclass image classification on the Lego Minifigs dataset.
The target variable was the minifig category, and categories with fewer than 20 examples were removed beforehand.

The model reached a best validation macro-F1 of 0.3943 and a corresponding validation accuracy of 0.6438.
When evaluated on the hold-out test set, the model achieved:
- Test accuracy: 0.6348
- Test macro-F1: 0.4100

These results indicate that the baseline CNN learns useful visual patterns from the images and generalizes reasonably well to unseen data.
The gap between training and validation/test performance suggests mild overfitting, but not severe collapse.

CLASS-LEVEL OBSERVATIONS

The best-performing categories were typically visually distinctive or internally consistent.
Examples include Vikings, LEGOLAND, Belville, Games, Technic, Friends, DUPLO, Town, and Harry Potter.
These classes likely benefit from recognizable colors, outfits, accessories, or overall design language.

The weakest classes were often categories with very few examples in the test set, such as Ninja, Western, Stranger Things, and Sonic the Hedgehog.
Several classes also obtained an F1-score of 0.0, meaning the model failed to correctly retrieve them at all.
This shows that the baseline struggles with rare classes and with categories that have substantial visual overlap with other themes.

ERROR ANALYSIS

Visual inspection of test predictions showed that the model often confuses categories that look stylistically similar.
For example, some Studios and Racers examples were predicted as Town, while some Castle or Super Heroes figures were confused with categories such as NINJAGO or Star Wars.
The model performed better on iconic franchise-based minifigs than on more generic human figures.

DISCUSSION

Overall, the baseline CNN provides a meaningful starting point for the assignment.
It demonstrates that image-based category prediction is feasible, but also that multiclass classification over many Lego themes remains challenging.
The main limitations of the baseline are:
1. difficulty with rare classes,
2. confusion between visually similar themes,
3. limited representational power compared with modern pretrained image models.

A logical next step is therefore to compare this baseline with a pretrained convolutional architecture such as EfficientNet or ResNet.
